Learn Chess

March 8th, 2023

Completely new to Chess, dont know where to start? Start Here.

Chess is a two-player game played on a board of 64 squares, with each player starting with 16 pieces. The aim of the game is to put your opponent's king in checkmate, which means the king is under attack and cannot escape capture on the next move. Here's how to play:

The board is set up so that each player has a row of eight pawns in front of them, and their other pieces are placed behind the pawns. The pieces are as follows: each player has one king, one queen, two rooks, two knights, and two bishops. The player with the white pieces goes first. They move one of their pieces to a different square on the board, following the rules for that particular piece. The other player then moves one of their pieces, and so on, with each player taking turns. Each piece moves in a different way. Here's a quick summary of each piece's movement:

Pawns: Move forward one or two squares on their first move, and one square forward on subsequent moves. They can only capture diagonally.

Rooks: Move any number of squares horizontally or vertically.

Knights: Move in an L-shape, two squares in one direction and then one square perpendicular to that.

Bishops: Move any number of squares diagonally.

Queen: Move any number of squares horizontally, vertically, or diagonally.

King: Move one square in any direction. If a player's piece lands on a square occupied by an opponent's piece, they capture that piece and remove it from the board. The game continues until one player's king is in checkmate, or if the game is a draw for some other reason.


Basic Rules

1. Check occurs when the king is attacked by an opposing piece.

2. Checkmate occurs when the king is in check and there is no legal move to get out of check.

3. Stalemate occurs when the player whose turn it is to move has no legal move, but their king is not in check.

4. Castling is a move where the king and rook are moved simultaneously, with the king moving two squares towards the rook and the rook moving to the square over which the king crossed.

5. En passant is a capture that can occur when a pawn moves two squares on its first move and ends up next to an opposing pawn.

6. Promotion occurs when a pawn reaches the opponent's back rank and can be replaced with a queen, rook, bishop, or knight.

7. The game can end in a draw if the same position is repeated three times or if no capture or pawn move has occurred in the last 50 moves.

8. Touch-move rule: If a player touches a piece, they must move it if it is a legal move. If they touch an opposing piece, they must capture it if it is a legal move.

9. Illegal moves result in a penalty, such as adding time to the opponent's clock or forfeiting the game.
