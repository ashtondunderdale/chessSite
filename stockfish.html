Stockfish - Chess Engine

Stockfish is one of the most popular chess engines in the world. It is a free and open-source chess engine that is used by many chess players, programmers, and chess enthusiasts around the world. Stockfish is a chess engine that is designed to analyze and play chess games at a very high level. It is used by many chess players as a training tool and to analyze their own games. The engine is also used in computer chess competitions to test its performance against other chess engines.

Stockfish was first created in 2008 by Tord Romstad, Marco Costalba, and Joona Kiiski. It is based on another open-source chess engine called Glaurung, which was also developed by Tord Romstad. Stockfish was created as a result of the frustration of the developers with the commercial chess engine programs that were available at that time. The aim was to create a high-quality chess engine that was free and open-source. Stockfish has gone through several versions since its creation in 2008. The current version of Stockfish is Stockfish 15.1, as of January 2023, Stockfish 15.1 achieved a FIDE rating of 3582, making it the strongest chess engine in the world.

Stockfish has achieved many breakthroughs over the years. In 2013, Stockfish won the TCEC Season 6 Superfinal, which was a major achievement for the engine. In the same year, Stockfish won the Chess.com Computer Chess Championship, which is another major computer chess tournament. Stockfish has won many other computer chess tournaments since then.

If you are interested in the technical stuff.. Stockfish can use up to 1024 CPU threads in multiprocessor systems. The maximal size of its transposition table is 32TB. Stockfish has been developing and tested on a framework called Fishtest , where volunteers can donate CPU time for tetsing and improvements for thie program. The various changes to the game playing code are accepted or rejected based on the results of tens of thousands of games on the framework against an older "reference" version of the program, utilising sequential probability ratio testing. The chi-squared test is used to verify the tests on the framework, only if the results are deemed statistically significant are they reliable and used in the software code.
